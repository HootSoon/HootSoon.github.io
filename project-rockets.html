Reusable Model Rocketry

CAD | 3D Printing | Aerodynamics & Simulation

← Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

Combining additive manufacturing with aerospace principles, I designed, simulated, and manufactured custom, reusable 3D printed model rockets of various sizes. Beyond just launching them myself, I scaled the operation into an online storefront, successfully selling these highly modular designs to other hobbyists on eBay.

Mechanical & Modular Design

A key focus of this project was engineering the rockets to be highly modular and durable enough for repeated launches, cutting down on the waste typical of single-use cardboard and balsa wood kits.

  • Modular Body Tubes: Modeled the chassis in Fusion360 to feature interchangeable body tube segments, allowing users to introduce larger payload sections to the basic rocket frame depending on mission requirements.
  • Replaceable Engine Sleeves: Engineered removable rocket engine sleeves for the lower section. Instead of a catastrophic failure ruining the entire rocket if an engine mount melts or cracks, the sleeve can simply be swapped out for full reusability.
  • Recovery Systems: Designed and integrated appropriate internal hooks, anchor points, and shock cord connections into the 3D models to ensure reliable parachute-based recovery.

Simulation & Aerodynamics

Before any filament was extruded, every design went through rigorous virtual testing to ensure stable and predictable flight paths.

  • Flight Profiling: Utilized OpenRocket to simulate launch physics, calculate expected apogee, and determine the optimal delay charges for parachute deployment.
  • Stability Margin: Modeled the Center of Gravity (CG) versus the Center of Pressure (CP) across various motor configurations to guarantee aerodynamic stability off the launch pad.
