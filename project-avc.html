Project Overview

Our team competed in the statewide Autonomous Vehicle Challenge, securing 2nd place in the 2024-2025 season and taking 1st place in 2025-2026. The competition required the vehicle to navigate dynamic environments and interact with objects while avoiding ever-changing distractions.

Hardware & Kinematics

A major component of our success was the physical interaction system. I worked on integrating the RO-ARM m3 pro robotic arm into the chassis.

  • Engineered a custom inverse kinematics (IK) solver in C++ to precisely control the robotic arm's end-effector.
  • Modeled mounting hardware in Fusion360 to integrate the sensor payload.

Software & Computer Vision

To navigate the field and score points, the robot needed reliable vision and localization.

  • Object Detection: Trained a custom YOLO v11 AI model deployed on an NVIDIA Jetson to detect colored balls and buckets in real-time.
  • Localization: Implemented a Monte-Carlo particle filter using an occupancy map to predict and track the robot's pose on the field.
  • Telemetry: Built a custom video feed and sensor visualization dashboard to monitor system health during runs.
