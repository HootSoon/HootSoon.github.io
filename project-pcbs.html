Project Overview

Transitioning from breadboards to custom-manufactured hardware is essential for creating reliable, scalable engineering projects. I use KiCad and LTspice to design, simulate, and lay out specialized printed circuit boards (PCBs). By integrating ground plane via stitching and generating automated bills of materials (BOM), I ensure my board layouts are optimized directly for actual manufacturing and assembly workflows.

Tool: KiCad EDA
Simulation: LTspice
Manufacturing: LCSC / JLCPCB
Architecture: 2-Layer & 4-Layer Stacks

Iterative Design: The ATtiny85 Counter

One of my core hardware projects involved building a dedicated counter circuit. To ensure maximum stability and optimize the footprint, the board underwent several strict iterations.

Hardware-Based Logic to Embedded Microcontroller

The earliest revisions of the board relied on purely hardware-based logic gates to perform the counting operations. While functional, the component density was unnecessarily high, which increased the risk of trace failure and manufacturing cost.

In the final iteration, I completely overhauled the architecture to migrate the counting logic onto an ATtiny85 microcontroller. This transition allowed me to:

  • Drastically reduce the physical footprint of the PCB.
  • Minimize the external component count (BOM) for cheaper manufacturing.
  • Introduce software-based debouncing for much cleaner signal inputs.
  • Allow the counting logic to be reprogrammed or updated via ICSP headers without fabricating a new board.

Advanced Power Distribution

Beyond logic circuits, I have designed 4-layer power distribution boards intended to handle high current loads safely while mitigating electromagnetic interference (EMI). These boards form the backbone of complex, motor-heavy systems like autonomous vehicles.

Ground Planes & Via Stitching

To ensure clean power delivery to sensitive 3.3v and 5v logic systems alongside noisy motor controllers, the board utilizes a dedicated 4-layer stackup. I implemented extensive via stitching across the continuous ground planes. This technique significantly lowers ground impedance, increases the thermal mass to dissipate heat away from the regulators, and provides shorter return paths to reduce signal noise.

Automated Manufacturing (PCBA)

Designing a board is only half the process; getting it built efficiently is the other. My KiCad workflows include strict component selection using LCSC parts catalogs. I generate automated Bills of Materials (BOM) and Pick-and-Place (CPL) files, allowing for seamless turn-key PCBA (Printed Circuit Board Assembly) directly from the fabrication house.
